Board & Card Games

Board and card games are structured play activities that use physical pieces, cards, or dice to guide players toward specific goals. We create unplugged board and card games that help learners explore computer science, AI, and data science concepts through movement and hands-on play. Some games are designed for tables, while others are large floor games where children can move, jump, and interact with CS concepts no screens required.

All projects below are in development — official release coming soon!

Game Concepts in Development

1. CT Concept Cards

A collectible card game where each card represents a key computational thinking concept— such as sequencing, decomposition, abstraction, or debugging. Players draw, trade, and combine cards to complete real-world “challenge missions.”

Prototype testing planned for Spring 2026.

2. Data Science Board Game

A dice-and-cards data adventure game that teaches how data is collected, cleaned, and visualized. Players roll, analyze, and compete to make discoveries—learning data literacy while having fun.

Early sketches under review.

3. Simon Says: AI Edition

A card-based version of “Simon Says” that introduces children to machine learning patterns—follow the rules that AI “learns,” spot the biases, and adapt as the “model” evolves. Great for group movement and reflection.

Playtesting planned for elementary workshops.

4. The Hopping Board Game

A large-floor or outdoor movement game where children jump between AI concept tiles—like “Data,” “Pattern,” “Decision,” and “Feedback Loop.” Each hop unlocks a question or mini-challenge about how AI learns.

Design prototypes in progress.
