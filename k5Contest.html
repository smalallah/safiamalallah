K-5 Coding Contest

The K-5 Coding Contest is an annual event that invites elementary students to build creative, working projects while practicing problem-solving and computational thinking. Contestants code solutions to age-appropriate prompts and present their projects to judges.

Why Focus on K-5?

Early exposure to coding strengthens curiosity, logic, and creativity. By centering K-5 learners—who are often underserved in formal CS programs—the contest helps build a strong foundation and broadens participation in computing from the start.

2025 Contest Overview

Students will create a project that responds to an on-the-day prompt using Scratch or ScratchJr (grade-appropriate). Judges will evaluate clarity of idea, creativity, and how well the code addresses the prompt.

Prizes

  • 1st Place: PlayStation 5
  • 2nd Place: Nintendo Switch Lite
  • 3rd Place: Nintendo Switch Lite
  • All Participants: Participation gift

Eligibility & Rules

  • Open to students in grades K–5.
  • Projects built in Scratch or ScratchJr (grade-appropriate).
  • Individual entries only (no teams).
  • Contest prompt announced at the start of the event.
  • Parents/guardians must accompany and supervise their child for the entire event (not a drop-off event).

Coding Levels

  • Level 1: K–2 — ScratchJr
  • Level 2: Grades 3–4 — ScratchJr or Scratch
  • Level 3: Grade 5 — Scratch (or ScratchJr if preferred)

Schedule

  • 8:30 AM – 9:00 AM: Check-in & review
  • 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM: Coding time
  • 12:00 PM – 1:00 PM: Lunch (pizza provided)
  • 1:00 PM – 3:30 PM: Presentations & judging
  • 3:30 PM – 4:00 PM: Awards
